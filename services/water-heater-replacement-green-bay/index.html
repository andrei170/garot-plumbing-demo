Five Signs to Replace

Your water heater is telling you. Here is what to listen for.

Most water heater failures show three or four warning signs in the weeks before the tank lets go. Catching one of these saves a flooded basement and a same-day emergency replacement.

01

Hot water runs cold faster than it used to.

Sediment build-up at the bottom of the tank cuts effective capacity. A 50-gallon tank starts performing like a 30-gallon. Worth flushing first, replacing if it persists.

02

Water at the tap looks rusty or smells metallic.

The anode rod has been exhausted and the tank itself is starting to corrode from the inside. Replacement window is now, not later.

03

You hear popping or rumbling from the tank.

Sediment at the bottom is being heated and creating steam pockets. Inefficient, loud, and a sign the tank's life expectancy is shortening fast.

04

You see water around the base.

This is the one that turns into the basement-flood call. If the tank itself is leaking (not the connections at the top), replacement is required, usually same week.

05

The unit is more than ten to twelve years old.

Most residential water heaters last eight to twelve years in Wisconsin's hard water. Past twelve, you are running on borrowed time. Plan the replacement, do not wait for the failure.

How long does a water heater replacement take?
Most standard water heater replacements take 2 to 4 hours from arrival to hot water back on. If your water heater fails before noon, you usually have hot water restored the same day. We stock 40 and 50-gallon gas and electric units on every truck.
How much does water heater replacement cost in Green Bay?
Cost depends on tank size, fuel type, permit requirements, and any code-compliance work needed at install. We give a fixed-price quote before any work begins so you know the number before you say yes. No clock-watching, no end-of-job upcharges.
Should I replace or repair my water heater?
If the tank is over 10 years old, leaking, or showing rust at the base, replacement is almost always the better choice. If it is under 8 years old and the issue is a thermostat, heating element, or anode rod, repair is often cheaper. We give you the honest answer based on what we find on site.
Do you replace tankless water heaters?
Yes. Gas tankless installation, replacement, and service is part of what we do. Tankless units cost more up front but use less energy long-term and never run out of hot water during a guest weekend or a Wisconsin winter shower marathon.
What signs mean my water heater is failing?
Five signs: (1) hot water runs cold faster than it used to, (2) water at the tap looks rusty or smells metallic, (3) you hear popping or rumbling from the tank, (4) you see water around the base, (5) the unit is more than 10-12 years old. Any one of these is worth a call.
